About Me

I’m a performance engineer by profession and a systems thinker by instinct. I spend most of my time solving problems that sit at the intersection of technology, efficiency, and scale—whether that’s optimizing applications, designing testing frameworks, or figuring out how to make complex systems actually work in the real world.

I’m not interested in surface-level solutions. I like going deep—understanding how things behave under pressure, where they break, and how they can be made better. That mindset extends beyond work into everything I explore: AI, automation, personal productivity, and building tools that are actually useful, not just impressive on paper.I tend to question defaults. If something feels inefficient, overcomplicated, or blindly accepted, I’ll dig into it. Sometimes that leads to better systems. Sometimes it just leads to clarity—but both matter.

Lately, I’ve been exploring how AI and agent-driven systems can reshape the way we build, test, and scale software. Alongside that, I’m working toward creating independent, profitable tech projects—things that can stand on their own without noise or hype. Outside of tech, I’m someone who values clarity, independence, and intentional decisions. I prefer depth over volume—whether it’s conversations, work, or ideas.
